The Reppert Research Group

Welcome to the Reppert research group. We're a curiosity-driven research group working at the interface between chemistry, biology, and physics. Our research typically focuses on the related questions:

  • How can we use physical principles to understand biological processes?
  • How can we use biological tools to solve physical problems?

Specific research areas are described in more detail below.

Photosynthetic Light Harvesting

Photosynthesis is the energetic bedrock for virtually all life on earth. Yet a detailed molecular understanding of the process remains elusive. Our interests are in both understanding the structure-function relationship of native photosynthetic proteins and using that knowledge to design artificial and bioengineered systems.
