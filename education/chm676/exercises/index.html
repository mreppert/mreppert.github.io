Exercises

Exercises are posted as Jupyter Notebook files on Github. To view an assignment (without running it), click the corresponding "Github" link; to download, right-click on the "Source" link and choose the "Save Link As" option.

Submit homework via e-mail attachment(s) with the subject line "CHM676 Exercise Submission". Assignments are due at 11:59 PM on the date indicated.

To run Jupyter Notebook files, I suggest three options:

  • Open the CHM676 MyBinder repository. This will allow you to open and edit exercise files directly, without installing anything on your computer or signing into any service. However, (1) sometimes the session takes a long time to load, and (2) you must download your work to your local computer when you finish -- otherwise it will be deleted when your session expires!

  • Sign into diagrid with your Purdue Career credentials and use the free Jupyter Notebook tool. Files you save on diagrid will remain there after you close your session, so you can save/continue your work without downloading to your local computer.

  • Install Anaconda on your personal computer and edit Jupyter Notebook files (even offline) directly in your web browser. I strongly recommend this option if you intend to continue using Jupyter Notebooks after this course is completed.
